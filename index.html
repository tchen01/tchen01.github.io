research
I have done research with the softmattertheory group at Tufts. During summer 2016 I participated in the RIPS-HK program at HKUST.
I worked with the softmattertheory group to optimize a Monte Carlo simulation used to explore the behavior of balls constrained to the surfaces of ellipsoids under various conditions. By exploiting the nature of the particle-particle interactions and geometric constraints I was able to rewrite write a linear time n-body algorithm for our simulations. My code reduced the runtime for a large number of particles by more than 50 times.
In the RIPS program I worked for the startup ePropulsion designing and implementing an underwater acoustic communication protocol.
