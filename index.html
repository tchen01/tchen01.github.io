About Me

I'm Tyler Chen, Tufts class of 2017. I love knowing how things work which is why I'm studying physics, literally "knowledge of nature".

I grew up in Omaha Nebraska where I spent my childhood playing with legos, climbing trees, building forts and reading. I currently attend Tufts University in Medford Massachusetts, a few miles north of Boston.

Interests

Physics, Chinese, Spanish, Graphic Design, Mathematics, Electronics, Technology, Videogames, Engineering.

I find that each of these areas give me a unique way of understanding the world. My interest in Language and Design stems from my desire to understand how we, as humans, can communicate effectively. Mathematics is amazingly consistent and is an incredibly powerful tool that physics relies on in order to give direct insight into how the cosmos functions on the most fundamental levels. I find it fascinating that we can predict and model so many of the events in our universe.

In addition to my academic interests, I find electronics and technology to be fascinating. Its incredible that I can get on a game and play with people from Chile, Germany, China or Honduras without leaving my desk.

Hobbies

I fly things and ride bicycles.

RC (remote control) helicopters and planes combine my interest in technology with my lifelong fascination with flight. There is something wonderful knowing that you are in control of the machine that you spent hours putting together and testing. When flying, you are always second away from a crash; a pilot error, mistake in assembly or even bad luck will all end poorly. This creates a particularly thrilling experience.

Cycling can offer a similarly exciting experience but it is also a great way to relax and enjoy the world. There is something beautiful about the simplicity of a bicycle's extremely functional easily maintainable design. The efficiency of a bicycle is not paralleled by any simple vehicle making it one of the most ideal forms of transportation.
