About Me

I'm Tyler Chen, Tufts class of 2017.

I spent my childhood in Omaha Nebraska playing with legos, building forts, climbing trees, reading, and playing backyard football. I'm currently studying physics, math and studio art at Tufts University which is located just north of Boston.

Interests

Physics, Chinese, Spanish, Design, Mathematics, Electronics, Technology, Videogames, Engineering.

I am majoring in physics and math but my academic interests extend beyond those subjects. I also intend to minor in studio art and have been learning Chinese. I want to be able to hold an intelligent conversation with anyone I meet so I make it a priority to learn about more that just sciency things.

In addition to my academics, I find electronics and technology to be fascinating. Its incredible that I can get on a game and interact with people from Chile, Germany, China or Honduras, all without leaving my room.

Hobbies

I make things and sometimes fly them.

When I was young I was fascinated by airplanes and decided I wanted to be a pilot. I spent my time making planes and spaceships out of cardboard and legos. While the childhood dream of being a pilot has since been abandoned, I now build and fly remote control helicopters and airplanes.

I've also worked on some small coding projects, both with friends and alone. These are mostly for fun but occasionally something useful is created. Most recently I've been working on monospace a chrome extensions to display code in Facebook chat as it would be seen in a text editor.
